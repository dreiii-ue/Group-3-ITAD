Community Sharing & Waste Reduction

Share More.
Waste Less.

Olio connects neighbors with each other and with local businesses so surplus food and household items can be shared, not thrown away.

Olio

What is Olio?

The Local Sharing Revolution

Olio is a free sharing app that helps reduce waste in your local community by making it easy to pass on surplus food and household items to someone nearby who can use them.

Editor's Rating: 4.3 / 5

How To Use

Start Sharing in 6 Steps

1

Snap a Photo

Take a clear picture of the food or item you want to share with your neighbors.

2

Add a Description

Briefly describe the item and specify the pickup location and time frame.

3

List Your Item

Post your listing to the local "Feed" where people in your area can see it instantly.

4

Browse Nearby

Check the app regularly to see what free items your neighbors are giving away.

5

Request & Message

Found something you need? Send a request and arrange details via the secure in-app chat.

6

Safe Pickup

Meet up at the agreed time for a contactless or face-to-face handover.

App Capabilities

Core Features

Hyperlocal Feed

See only listings from people in your immediate area, making pickups quick and convenient.

Secure In-App Chat

Coordinate pickups safely without sharing personal contact details outside the platform.

Business Partnerships

Partner cafes, bakeries, and supermarkets list surplus food daily — often available for free or very cheap.

Item & Food Categories

Browse by food, household goods, clothes, plants, or books to quickly find exactly what you need.

Evaluation

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Completely free to use for both givers and receivers.
  • Includes both food and household item categories in one app.
  • Business partnerships mean a steady stream of fresh food listings daily.
  • Highly intuitive interface that anyone can use within minutes of downloading.

Cons

  • Limited usefulness in low-density rural or suburban areas with fewer users.
  • Food safety relies entirely on the honesty of the person listing items.
  • Popular items go quickly and may require frequent checking to not miss out.
  • No built-in quality verification for listed items before pickup.

Verdict

Final Review

Olio is a beautifully simple concept executed exceptionally well. In areas with an active user base, it delivers a constant stream of free food and goods that would otherwise be wasted, making it simultaneously great for your wallet and the environment. The app is frictionless enough that anyone can start contributing within minutes of downloading. Its main limitation is a dependence on local adoption, but in cities and towns where it has taken hold, it has become an indispensable part of community life. Highly recommended for environmentally conscious households.
